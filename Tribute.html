Swami Vivekanandan


Swami Vivekananda, a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world, played a crucial role in the revival of Hinduism in India. His inspirational speeches and teachings continue to influence and inspire people around the globe.

About The Legend

  • Swami Vivekananda was born as Narendranath Datta on January 12, 1863, in Kolkata, India
  • He was a brilliant student and had a keen interest in spirituality from a young age.
  • Vivekananda became a disciple of the saint Ramakrishna, who taught him that all living beings were an embodiment of the divine self.
  • He is best known for his speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893, where he began his speech with "Sisters and brothers of America..."
  • Vivekananda founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897, which works in the areas of education, healthcare, and social service.
  • His teachings focused on the potential divinity of every individual and the idea of serving humanity as a way to serve God.
  • Swami Vivekananda's books on the four Yogas (Raja Yoga, Karma Yoga, Bhakti Yoga, and Jnana Yoga) are highly regarded and widely read.
  • He passed away on July 4, 1902, at the young age of 39, but his legacy continues to live on through his teachings and the institutions he established.
